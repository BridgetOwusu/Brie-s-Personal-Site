Lab Work

Circuit Analysis
Labs

Lab work is where theory becomes real. These are the hands-on experiments from ELC 109 — where I built circuits, measured values, and learned to trust the math.

Each lab reinforced a core principle: in engineering, precision is not optional. Every calculation, every measurement, every result has to account for why.

Lab 01
Ohm's Law Verification

Built and measured resistive circuits to verify the relationship between voltage, current, and resistance. Calculated theoretical vs. measured values and analyzed percent error.

V = IR
Lab 02
Series Circuit Analysis

Analyzed multi-resistor series circuits, measured total resistance, voltage drops across each component, and verified series current consistency throughout the loop.

Series Circuits
Lab 03
Kirchhoff's Voltage Law

Applied KVL to multi-source circuits. Wrote and solved loop equations, verified that the algebraic sum of voltages around any closed loop equals zero.

KVL
